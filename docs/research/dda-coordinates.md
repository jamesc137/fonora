# Meaning from coordinates: the Gen 3 DDA experiment

> **TL;DR.** Gen 3 inverted the problem entirely. Instead of starting from English concepts and assigning sounds, it asked what meaning *emerges* from the articulation grid itself. Meaning became a position in three axes — Depth, Mode, Aspect — and everyday concepts like "river" appeared only as derivations, never as primitives. It was the project's most intellectually pure phase, and it was later demoted on purpose.

## The question

What kinds of meaning naturally emerge from the Fonora articulation grid if you assume you have *no prior word* for "water" — no English to lean on at all?

## The hypothesis

Meaning is a coordinate. Every primitive is a point in ⟨Depth, Mode, Aspect⟩ (DDA), read off the articulatory grid. Familiar concepts are never primitives; they appear only at the *derivation* layer, composed from grid-native pieces.

## The constraints

- A small set of **grid-native primitives** (around 36), each explainable from its ⟨D, M, A⟩ position **without English**.
- **No** Indo-European lexicon repair — the whole point was to avoid borrowing.
- Throat gaps in the grid force coordinate rotation rather than invented symbols.

## What we built

[`tools/fonoran-gen3.js`](https://github.com/jamesc137/fonora/blob/main/tools/fonoran-gen3.js) turned coordinates into CV roots, with the full theory written up in the 460-line [DDA Gen 3 archive](../fonoran-gen3.md). The output is browsable in the [Dictionary](/language#dictionary).

## What happened

The inversion was real and demonstrable — *river* became flow + path = **xaeli**, assembled from emanation/flux/turbulent and index/passage/structure. For a time Gen 3 held experimental authority over grid-native semantics. But the words clustered phonetically, and — more fundamentally — the approach optimized *coordinate correctness*, which is not the same thing as *communication*. The Constitution later demoted the entire DDA track.

## The question that followed

Gen 3 roots were coherent but hard to tell apart by ear (seven words sharing an `-ee` rhyme class). Could we keep the DDA semantics untouched while making the words actually *memorable* to a human learner?
