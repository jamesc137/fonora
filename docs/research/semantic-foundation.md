# The irreducible dimensions of meaning

> **TL;DR.** After several generators that assigned sounds first, this experiment reversed the order: figure out *meaning* before touching phonetics. It proposed around 99 primitive dimensions of reality — chosen by a "fundamental experience test" — kept grammar separate from the lexicon, and built compounds hierarchically instead of as flat chains. Phonetics were deliberately deferred.

## The question

The previous generators all started from English words or grid coordinates. What are the actual *dimensions of reality* — not English vocabulary — from which compounds could write themselves?

## The hypothesis

There is a small set of irreducible semantic primitives, identifiable by a **fundamental experience test**: a concept is primitive only if it *cannot be naturally expressed using simpler Fonoran concepts*. This is a test of expressibility, not a toddler word list.

## The constraints

- **Phonetics deferred** — sound assignment is a later phase, not part of choosing primitives.
- **Grammar separated** from roots: relational machinery becomes particles, not lexical entries.
- **Hierarchical compounding** — `tribe = community + identity`, not a flat chain of three roots.
- Demote vague abstractions (walk, useful, cause, effect, void); elevate structural ones (thing, change, move, equal, bond, empty).

## What we built

[`data/fonoran-semantic-primitives.json`](https://github.com/jamesc137/fonora/blob/main/data/fonoran-semantic-primitives.json) (99 dimensions), a grammar-particles file, and 50 phonetics-free demo compounds, all set out in the [semantic foundation](../fonoran-semantic-foundation.md) proposal. It explicitly supersedes the [Huffman allocation](/research/notes/huffman-roots) and diagnoses what every prior generator got wrong. The concepts are now editable in the [Concept Editor](/language#concepts).

## What happened

The semantic inventory was adopted as the project's reference for *meaning*. Just as importantly, the document named the recurring failure modes — English-ranked lemmas, phonetics-first ordering, flat concatenation, grammar-as-roots, and auto-import without human review — so they would not recur.

## The question that followed

We now had a principled set of meanings. But a deeper question loomed over the whole enterprise: how do we *judge success*? Is Fonoran trying to be an ontologically correct system, or a language two people can actually communicate in?
